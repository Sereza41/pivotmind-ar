Phase 01 — Awareness
You notice the trigger.
Look around. The smell of alcohol, familiar sounds, the warmth of a crowd. Your brain is responding to environmental cues — conditioned over time. This is a learned reflex. Not a genuine need.
Phase 02 — Recognition
Name what you feel.
Notice the urge without acting on it. Where do you feel it in your body? Chest? Throat? Hands? This sensation is temporary — it will peak and pass within minutes. You are not your craving.
Phase 03 — Reframe
Separate the story from the fact.
Your mind says "one drink won't hurt." That's the addiction speaking — not reality. The truth: alcohol never delivered what it promised. Relief was temporary. The cost was real. You know this.
Phase 04 — Urge Surfing
Ride the wave.
Cravings are like waves — they build, peak, and break on their own. You don't need to fight it or give in. Just observe it. Breathe through it. The wave is already beginning to fall. You're still here.
Phase 05 — Integration
You chose yourself.
You stayed present in a high-risk environment and did not break. Each session like this rewires your brain — weakening the trigger response, building new neural pathways. This is real, biological change.
